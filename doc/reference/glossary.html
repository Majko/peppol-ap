📘 Peppol Glossary

All terms used in this teaching series. Kept consistent across lessons.

4-Corner Model
The Peppol network topology: Buyer (C1) → Sending AP (C2) → Receiving AP (C3) → Seller (C4). No direct connections between end-users; all traffic flows through certified Access Points.
Access Point (AP)
The server software that sends and receives Peppol business documents on behalf of end-users. Must be certified through OpenPeppol and a Peppol Authority. You will build this.
AS4
The transport protocol used in Peppol. A profile of ebMS 3.0 that defines how messages are packaged, signed, encrypted, and delivered over HTTPS. Stands for "Applicability Statement 4."
BIS — Business Interoperability Specification
OpenPeppol's specification that defines a specific business document (e.g., BIS Billing 3.0 for invoices). Includes the data model, XML schema, Schematron rules, and code lists.
BIS Billing 3.0
The current mandatory Peppol specification for invoices and credit notes. Based on the European standard EN 16931. Uses UBL 2.1 XML or CII syntax. This is what you'll implement.
C1 / C2 / C3 / C4
Shorthand for the four corners: C1 = Buyer/End User, C2 = Sending AP, C3 = Receiving AP, C4 = Seller/End User.
CII — Cross-Industry Invoice
An alternative XML syntax for e-invoices (ISO/TS 20022-5). Peppol BIS Billing supports both UBL 2.1 and CII. UBL is more common in practice.
CIUS — Core Invoice Usage Specification
A national or sector-specific subset/rules on top of EN 16931. Slovakia may publish a CIUS for Slovak-specific invoicing requirements.
ebMS 3.0
OASIS standard "ebXML Messaging Services 3.0." AS4 is a profile of this standard. Defines how messages are packaged, routed, and acknowledged.
EN 16931
The European standard for e-invoicing. Defines the semantic data model that all EU member states must accept. Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 is a CIUS of EN 16931.
OpenPeppol
The international non-profit association (AISBL, Brussels) that owns and governs the Peppol specifications and the Peppol Network. All service providers must be members.
PASR — Peppol Authority Specific Requirements
Additional rules that a national Peppol Authority (e.g., Slovak Financial Directorate) imposes within its jurisdiction. In Slovakia, this includes a centralised SMP and Slovak TDD (technical design document).
Peppol Authority (PA)
A national body delegated by OpenPeppol to oversee Peppol implementation in a country. For Slovakia: Financial Directorate of the Slovak Republic (Finančné riaditeľstvo SR). Contact: efaktura@financnasprava.sk
Peppol Network
The collective infrastructure of certified Access Points, SMPs, and the SML, governed by OpenPeppol rules. Not a physical network — it's a legal and technical framework.
PKI Certificate
X.509 certificate used for signing and encrypting AS4 messages. Must be obtained through the Peppol Service Desk from an approved Certificate Authority. Two types: signing/encryption certificate (Peppol-issued) and TLS certificate (any trusted CA).
SBDH — Standard Business Document Header
The XML envelope that wraps every Peppol business document. Contains sender ID, receiver ID, document type (e.g., urn:oasis:names:specification:ubl:schema:xsd:Invoice-2::Invoice##urn:cen.eu:en16931:2017##compliant#urn:fdc:peppol.eu:2017:poacc:billing:3.0::2.1), and instance identifier.
SML — Service Metadata Locator
A DNS-based root directory operated by OpenPeppol. Given a participant ID, it returns the SMP URL for that participant. You don't build this.
SMP — Service Metadata Publisher
A directory service that publishes what document types a participant can receive and which AP handles their traffic. You may need to operate one for your customers (required by Slovak PASR).
SPA — Service Provider Agreement
The legal contract between a Peppol Authority and a service provider. Governs the rights and obligations of operating an AP.
Schematron
A rule-based validation language. Peppol BIS specs include Schematron rules that go beyond XSD validation — e.g., "if VAT category is 'standard', VAT rate must be 23%" (Slovak VAT rate).
Testbed
OpenPeppol's automated testing environment. New service providers must pass Testbed scenarios to prove their AP correctly sends/receives BIS-compliant documents.
TLS Certificate
Separate from the Peppol PKI certificate. Used for HTTPS. Can be from any well-known CA (Let's Encrypt, etc.). Self-signed certificates are NOT allowed in production.
UBL — Universal Business Language
An OASIS standard XML vocabulary for business documents (ISO/IEC 19845). Peppol BIS uses UBL 2.1. Key document types: Invoice, CreditNote, Order, etc.
ViDA — VAT in the Digital Age
An EU initiative mandating digital real-time VAT reporting. Drives the 2027 Slovakia mandate and the 2030 cross-border expansion.
