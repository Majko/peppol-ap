1. The Slovak Market — Context

From 1 January 2027, all VAT-registered Slovak businesses must issue and receive structured electronic invoices for domestic B2B transactions via the Peppol network. PDF invoices will no longer be legally valid for in-scope transactions. Slovakia uses a decentralised 5-corner model where the tax authority (Finančná správa SR) sits at corner 5, receiving reporting data directly from certified Digital Postmen (CPDS).

This affects roughly ~350,000 VAT-registered entities in Slovakia. Every one of them needs a certified Digital Postman by 1 Jan 2027.

As of June 2026, there are 38 certified providers on the Finančná správa SR list. These range from:

  • Local Slovak ERP firms adding Peppol as a module (Pohoda, Money S3, KROS)
  • Big-4 advisory firms (Deloitte, KPMG, PwC, EY)
  • Global e-invoicing platforms (Pagero/Thomson Reuters, Basware, Tradeshift, Qvalia)
  • Dedicated digital postmen (ePošťák, Fakturix, Doklado, ESO9, Asseco)
  • White-label infrastructure providers (Tickstar, ecosio)
📊 Key stat: Only 5 out of 38 providers have fully public pricing in EUR (Asseco, Doklado, Billit, Qvalia, ESO9). Another ~2 have partially available pricing (Flowis, Pagero/Thomson Reuters). The remaining ~31 require you to contact their sales team. This means transparent pricing is itself a competitive advantage.

3. OpenPeppol Membership Costs (Your Cost Base)

To operate a Peppol AP, you must become an OpenPeppol member and pay annual fees. These are based on company size:

CategoryYour sizeSign-up feeAnnual fee (candidate)Annual fee (certified)
AP only, S1 1–10 employees €1,025 €1,800 €2,800
AP only, S2 11–50 employees €1,025 €1,800 €2,800
AP only, S3 51–250 employees €2,100 €2,900 €3,900
AP + SMP, S1 1–10 employees €1,750 €2,650 €4,150

Additional one-time costs:

  • Certification fee (upon first certification): €2,500 (for AP + SMP, billed once, then added to annual fee thereafter)
  • Peppol PKI certificate: issued by OpenPeppol CA — free for members (included in membership)

Total first-year cost (AP only, S1 size): €1,025 (sign-up) + €1,800 (candidate annual) + €2,500 (certification) = €5,325

Ongoing annual cost (certified, AP only): €2,800

⚠️ Important: The Slovak Peppol Authority (Finančná správa SR) may impose additional national fees or requirements beyond OpenPeppol. The accreditation procedure PDF mentions specific Slovak requirements — factor in ~€500–1,000 for Slovak-specific legal/admin costs in year one.

6. Revenue Model — Proposed Pricing

Our positioning: Pure AP Core as a Service

Unlike the competitors above, we're not building a full invoicing portal with AI OCR, bank integrations, and inventory management. We're building a pure, high-performance Peppol transport layer — the certified AS4 engine — exposed via REST API and webhooks. Our customers are:

  • Accounting firms that already have their own invoicing software and just need Peppol connectivity
  • ERP vendors (Pohoda, Money S3, KROS, Omega integrators) who need a white-label Peppol backend
  • Software developers building multi-tenant platforms who want to embed Peppol without certification themselves
  • Mid-sized SMEs with existing accounting setups who don't want to switch to a full invoicing platform

⚠️ Critical dependency: This analysis assumes no full-time employees beyond the founder. If you need to hire (even one developer or support person at ~€2,000/mo), the break-even shifts dramatically:
  • With 1 hire: break-even at ~130 SMEs (~€3,900/mo revenue)

Keep the operation lean until revenue justifies headcount. The AP Core is designed to be low-touch — automate as much as possible.

9. Competitive Differentiation Strategy

With 38 certified providers, you can't win on price alone (€8/mo floor is already set). Here's how we differentiate:

DimensionCurrent competitorsOur advantage
API quality Most competitors bundle Peppol with a full invoicing UI. Their APIs are secondary. API-first by design. Our entire product is a REST API. Webhooks, webhook HMAC signing, idempotency, deterministic error codes. Developer experience is the product.
White-label Only PeppolEDGE and Tickstar offer true white-label. They're expensive (€250+). White-label from day one. ERP integrators (Pohoda, Money, Omega) resell under their brand. No UI from us — just the API and SMP registration.
Latency + reliability Many competitors run single-server, single-region setups. Active-active multi-region (from Phase 3). Better SLA than most Slovak competitors who are on single Hetzner boxes.
Transparent pricing 31 of 38 providers hide their pricing behind sales calls. Full public pricing on the website. No "contact sales" gate. Credit card sign-up, self-service activation.
SMP ownership Most providers use a shared SMP or don't offer SMP services. We run our own SMP. If a customer wants their own SMP entry under their own brand, we provide it (White-Label tier).
Open source core Most competitors use closed-source stacks (Oxalis-NG is open but Java). Node.js + open-source AS4. Customers can inspect the transport layer. No Java dependency. Easy to extend.

11. Strategic Recommendations

  1. Start with the API Developer (free) tier immediately. Get developers and integrators testing in the sandbox. They'll become your sales channel.
  2. Target accounting firms, not end-SMEs, in Month 1–6. One accounting firm managing 50 clients = 50 companies on your platform. Far cheaper to acquire than 50 individual SMEs.
  3. Publish pricing on Day 1. Being one of the few providers with transparent public pricing is a genuine differentiator. It builds trust and reduces sales friction.
  4. Run MVP infrastructure (Phase 2) for at least the first 6 months post-launch. Don't jump to active-active (Phase 3) until you have 200+ paying companies. The cost difference is 10×.
  5. Budget ~€8,000 for Year 1 all-in (accreditation + infrastructure + legal). Revenue from 100+ SMEs covers this. After that, it's pure margin.
  6. Don't build a UI. The competitive advantage is API + white-label. Every euro spent on a customer portal is a euro not spent on API quality, docs, or SMP reliability. Let your partners build the UI.
  7. Monitor the 31 "price hidden" competitors. If any of them publish pricing below €8/mo, we may need to adjust. But for now, the market floor is stable.
