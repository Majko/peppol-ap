1. Why a White-Label Bridge?

While we pursue our own Peppol AP certification (10–14 weeks), we can immediately offer Peppol connectivity by reselling a certified provider's infrastructure under our own brand. This is a standard offering from several Peppol infrastructure providers — they handle the certified transport layer, we handle the customer relationship, API, and support.

⏱️ Why do this? Certification takes 10–14 weeks. The white-label bridge lets us:
  • Start signing up customers now, not in October
  • Generate revenue that covers certification costs (~€6,000)
  • Test our API, onboarding, and support processes with real customers
  • Build the customer base before the January 2027 rush
  • Migrate customers seamlessly to our own AP later (same API, same SMP entry)

2. Slovak Legal Requirements — What the Law Says

Before evaluating providers, it's critical to understand what the Slovak Peppol Authority (PA SK) legally requires for registering end customers. We analysed Stormware Pohoda's conditions and traced each requirement back to the official PA SK documents.

2.1 The Three Layers of Onboarding

Based on the Finančná správa SR FAQ and the PA SK accreditation scheme, there are three distinct layers when onboarding an SME:

LayerWhat it meansWho handles it
Authorization Confirmation that the AP provider has authorization from the tax subject (the SME). The FS app verifies the SME exists as a taxpayer. Finančná správa app + CPDS
KYC Identity verification — who is the statutory representative, who can approve API keys, who can change providers. This is entirely the provider's responsibility. CPDS (us) — our choice of process
SMP registration Technical publishing of the SME's Peppol metadata (participant ID, endpoint, certificate) in the centralised Slovak SMP so they can receive invoices. CPDS in the central Slovak SMP

2.2 What Is Legally Required (from PA SK / Finančná správa)

RequirementSourceBinding?
Each SME must have their own Peppol Participant ID using the scheme 0245:DIČ (10-digit tax ID, e.g. 0245:1234567890). PASR Point 4: "End User identification scheme — The scheme ID 0245:XXXXXXXXXX must be used when registering Slovakian End Users." ✅ YES — legal requirement
Each SME must be registered in the centralised Slovak SMP. You cannot register them in a foreign or private SMP. PASR Point 4: "Service Providers must register or ensure that all Slovakian End users are registered on the centralised SMP." ✅ YES — legal requirement
The provider must have PA SK accreditation before offering services to Slovak end users. PASR Point 7.1: "The Service Provider must apply for accreditation [...] before they may offer services to End Users established in Slovakia." ✅ YES — legal requirement
Each SME must have a legal relationship with the provider. The form of this relationship is not prescribed. Implied by KYC responsibility. The FS FAQ says KYC is the provider's responsibility. ✅ Required — but form is flexible
When switching providers, the old provider must deregister the SME from the centralised SMP within 3 working days. PASR Point 5: "When a Slovakian End User decides to switch [...] the existing Service Provider must deregister the End User from the centralised SMP within 3 working days." ✅ YES — legal requirement

2.3 What Is NOT Legally Required (Stormware's Business Policy)

Stormware Pohoda's published conditions state that for an accounting firm to issue invoices on behalf of a client:

  • A separate contract between Stormware and each SME client is required
  • Each client must have their own Peppol ID and communication tokens tied to their IČO
  • The client must consent to Stormware's T&Cs

We traced each of these against the official PA SK and FS documents. Here's the verdict:

Stormware's conditionLegal basis?Verdict
Separate contract between Stormware and each SME The PASR says provider must have a legal relationship with end users, but does not mandate a separate physical contract per SME. Clickwrap T&Cs during self-service onboarding are legally sufficient. ❌ Stormware's choice
You can use digital clickwrap
Communication tokens tied to specific IČO No legal requirement for per-SME tokens. The FS FAQ says the provider is responsible for KYC — how you implement it technically is up to you. ❌ Stormware's technical design
You can use accounting-firm-level tokens with per-SME delegation
All three conditions must be met before sending PA SK doesn't prescribe implementation details — it says end users must be identifiable and registered in the SMP. ❌ Stormware's risk-averse interpretation
🔑 The key distinction: 
LEGAL REQUIREMENT (must):
  Each SME → Peppol ID 0245:DIČ → registered in central SK SMP
  Each invoice → identifies the actual SME sender/receiver

STORMWARE'S INTERPRETATION (their choice):
  Each SME → separate paper contract + individual token + consent

YOUR OPTION (also valid):
  Each SME → Peppol ID 0245:DIČ → registered in central SK SMP
           → clickwrap T&Cs during self-service onboarding
           → accounting firm manages via API with per-SME delegation
           → No separate paper contract needed per SME

The only non-negotiable: each SME must have their own 0245:DIČ participant ID in the centralised Slovak SMP. You cannot batch multiple SMEs under one ID.

These are not mutually exclusive. The optimal approach is to use Model A now while pursuing your own cert in parallel:

✅ This way you get:
  1. Speed: Customers in 2–4 weeks via Tickstar's shared cert. Revenue from Month 2.
  2. Ownership: Your own cert arrives in Month 4–5. No rush — you already have paying customers.

  4. No GoRoute-style delay: You don't wait for your own cert before starting.

5. Selection Criteria — What to Evaluate

CriterionWhy it mattersWhat to ask
Certificate ownership Do we get our own Peppol cert or operate under theirs? "Will the cert be issued to our company name?"
SMP ownership Who controls the SMP entry? Can we transfer entries later? "Can participants be registered under our provider ID? Can entries be transferred?"

Participant registration model Can we register participants via API with per-SME identities? Or does each need a separate contract with you? "Can we register participants via API? Do you require a separate contract per end SME, or can we handle KYC ourselves?"
API compatibility Must map to our AP Core interface. "REST API? Webhook delivery? Can we send UBL XML via API?"
Slovak-specific compliance BIS 3.0 SK extension, IS EFA reporting, PA SK accreditation. "Slovak BIS 3.0 SK? IS EFA format? Slovak Peppol Authority compliance?"
Offboarding process Standard business continuity — how do participants get deregistered if we switch providers? "What is the process and typical lead time for deregistering participants from your SMP? What data export do you provide upon termination?"
SLA AS4 endpoint uptime, API uptime, support response. "Uptime SLA? Published SLA document? Incident response?"
Pricing model Per-participant, per-transaction, or flat fee. Limits and overage. "Pricing structure? Caps on participants or transactions? Overage cost?"
Data residency Must be EU (GDPR). Ideally within EU/EEA. "Infrastructure location? EU-only data residency guarantee?"
🤫 Strategic note — what to reveal vs what to keep internal:

Position yourself as an accounting / software service provider looking for a reliable Peppol AP partner for your ongoing business. Do not mention that you plan to operate your own certified AP in the future — this would give providers no incentive to offer good pricing, invest in onboarding, or support SMP portability.

The migration path questions (Section 8) should be framed as standard business continuity planning — "what happens if we want to switch providers in the future" — not as "we're building our own AP and leaving." Every professional customer asks about data export and provider switching. It's a normal procurement question.

What matters contractually is short cancellation terms (monthly or quarterly). If you have a 30-day cancellation clause, you can leave whenever you want regardless of what you said in the introductory email. The contract is what binds you, not the sales pitch.

7. Technical Integration — Adapter Layer

Our AP Core defines a clean interface. To use a white-label provider, build a thin adapter that translates between our interface and theirs:

┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ OUR SERVICE PLATFORM │ │ (API + portal — same whether white-label or our own AP) │ └────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ ▼ ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ AP CORE INTERFACE (unchanged) │ │ │ │ sendInvoice(ublXml, participantId) → messageId │ │ lookupParticipant(participantId) → endpoint + cert │ │ receiveWebhook(payload) → void │ │ getStatus(messageId) → status │ └────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ ▼ ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ ADAPTER LAYER (swappable) │ │ │ │ ┌─────────────────────┐ ┌──────────────────────────────────┐ │ │ │ White-label adapter │ │ Our own AP adapter (future) │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ Calls Tickstar API │ │ Calls our own AS4 engine │ │ │ │ Maps responses to │ │ (Node42) directly │ │ │ │ our internal format │ │ │ │ │ └──────────┬──────────┘ └──────────────────────────────────┘ │ │ │ │ └─────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘ │ ▼ ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ WHITE-LABEL PROVIDER (e.g. Tickstar) │ │ │ │ • Their certified AS4 endpoint │ │ • Their SMP with our participants under our provider ID │ │ • Their Peppol certificate (or ours, depending on model) │ │ • Their Testbed compliance + OpenPeppol certification │ └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Key principle: The Service Platform never knows whether it's talking to a white-label backend or our own AP. When we migrate, we swap the adapter — zero changes to the platform or customer-facing API.

8. Migration Path — White-Label → Our Own AP

  1. Preparation (weeks before migration): Register all new participants directly in our own SMP from Day 1 of our certification. Existing participants stay on the white-label SMP. Build and test the "our own AP" adapter in staging.
  2. Batch migration (single maintenance window): Schedule a low-traffic window (e.g. Saturday night). For each participant: update SMP entry to point to our AS4 endpoint. This is a DNS+metadata change — the participant's Peppol ID doesn't change. Per PASR, the old provider must deregister within 3 working days.
  3. Adapter swap: Deploy the "our own AP" adapter. The Service Platform continues using the same internal interface — the adapter just routes to Node42 instead of the white-label API. No change for end customers.
  4. Decommission white-label: Once all participants are migrated and verified, cancel the white-label contract. Total migration time: hours, not weeks. No need for lengthy coexistence.
🔑 Negotiate SMP portability upfront: Ensure the contract allows SMP entries to be transferred to your own SMP without requiring participants to re-register. Most reputable providers support this — confirm in writing. Frame this as: "What is your standard offboarding process for participants?" — a normal question any professional buyer would ask.
