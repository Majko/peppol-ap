What Peppol is, how the 4-corner model works, and what you need to become a service provider.

What is Peppol?

Peppol stands for Pan-European Public Procurement OnLine. It is both:

  1. An organisation (OpenPeppol AISBL) вҖ” a Brussels-based international association that manages the standards and governance.
  2. A network вҖ” a set of technical specifications, legal agreements, and certified service providers that enable businesses across Europe (and beyond) to exchange electronic business documents securely and interoperably.

Think of Peppol as the "email for business documents" вҖ” a standardised postal service where anyone on the network can send invoices, credit notes, purchase orders, and more to anyone else, regardless of what software they use.

рҹҮёрҹҮ° Why this matters for Slovakia: From 1 January 2027, all VAT-registered businesses in Slovakia must issue e-invoices for domestic B2B transactions вҖ” and they must use Peppol-based infrastructure. The government is replacing the current IS EFA system with a new Peppol-native solution. This creates a massive, time-limited market opportunity.

The 4-Corner Model

Peppol uses a 4-corner model. Instead of every company connecting directly to every other company (NВІ connections), they each connect to a certified Access Point:

The corners are role-agnostic вҖ” they only describe the direction of the document flow. Depending on the scenario:

Scenario C1 Sender C4 Receiver Document
B2G invoicing Supplier (your SME customer) рҹҸӣ Government agency Invoice
B2B invoicing Supplier (your SME customer) Buyer (another business) Invoice
Receiving invoices Supplier (your SME customer's vendor) Your SME customer (as buyer) Invoice
Government sending рҹҸӣ Government agency Supplier (your SME customer) Order / Contract

рҹҮёрҹҮ° So where is the government? In the most common case вҖ” an SME sending an invoice to the state вҖ” your customer is C1 and the government is C4. But your Access Point handles both directions: it sends documents from your customers (C1вҶ’C2) and receives documents addressed to them (C3вҶ’C4 via other APs).

The magic: you only need to integrate with one Access Point (Corner 2 or 3), and you can reach anyone else on the network вҖ” including every government agency that's connected via their AP. The Access Points handle the translation, validation, and secure delivery.

Your role as a Service Provider

You will operate as Corner 2 and/or Corner 3 вҖ” an Access Point that:

  • Sends documents from your customers (SMEs) to other Access Points on the network
  • Receives documents from other Access Points and delivers them to your customers
  • Validates documents against Peppol BIS rules before accepting them
  • Manages certificates (Peppol PKI) to prove you are a trusted participant

Key Components of the Peppol Network

1. Access Point (AP)

The core server software that sends and receives AS4 messages. This is what you will build. It handles:

  • AS4 messaging (secure document transport over HTTPS)
  • SBDH (Standard Business Document Header) вҖ” the envelope around every document
  • Digital signatures and message-level encryption using Peppol PKI certificates
  • Message receipt acknowledgements (receiving AP must confirm delivery)

2. Service Metadata Publisher (SMP)

A directory that publishes what documents each participant can receive and which AP to send them to. Think of it as DNS for business documents.

  • When AP-A wants to send an invoice to Participant-B, it first queries the SMP to find: "Which AP does Participant-B use? What document types do they accept? What are the routing rules?"
  • You may also operate an SMP for your customers (Slovak PASR may require this).

3. Service Metadata Locator (SML)

A DNS-based root directory that tells APs which SMP to query for a given participant. This is operated centrally by OpenPeppol вҖ” you don't build this.

The Standards Stack

Peppol invoices go through several layers. From bottom to top:

Layer Standard What it does
Document Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 (EN 16931) Defines the business data: invoice fields, VAT codes, party info. Uses UBL 2.1 XML or CII syntax.
Envelope SBDH (Standard Business Document Header) Wraps the business document with routing metadata: sender ID, receiver ID, document type, instance identifier.
Security Peppol PKI + AS4 security Digital signatures, encryption, non-repudiation. X.509 certificates issued by an approved Certificate Authority.
Transport AS4 (profile of ebMS 3.0) The actual protocol for sending/receiving messages. Uses SOAP with WSS over HTTPS. Reliable messaging with receipts.

What documents flow through Peppol?

Peppol covers a full procurement cycle, but for your MVP you only need the Post-Award documents (the invoicing phase):

  • Invoice (UBL Invoice) вҖ” the main event
  • Credit Note (UBL CreditNote) вҖ” correcting a previous invoice
  • Status Response вҖ” message-level receipts and business-level status (e.g., "invoice rejected")

вҡ  Focus: Start with just Invoice and Credit Note. The Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 spec defines the exact XML structure. You'll validate against Schematron rules and the UBL XML Schema.

What does it mean to be a Peppol Service Provider?

Legally, you must:

  1. Become an OpenPeppol member вҖ” pay annual fees based on company revenue tier.
  2. Sign the Peppol Service Provider Agreement with the Slovak Peppol Authority (Financial Directorate of the Slovak Republic вҖ” efaktura@financnasprava.sk).
  3. Pass interoperability testing in the OpenPeppol Testbed вҖ” they verify your AP correctly sends/receives BIS-compliant documents over AS4.
  4. Obtain Peppol PKI certificates вҖ” one for signing messages, one for TLS (the TLS one can be from any trusted CA).
  5. Register in the SMP вҖ” so other APs can discover your customers.
рҹӣ  Technically, you can build and test your AP before doing any of this (using self-signed certs and the Peppol test environment). The legal process runs in parallel. The 4-week prototype timeline focuses on the technical implementation first.
