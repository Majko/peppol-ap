🧾 Lesson 2: Anatomy of a Peppol Invoice

What a Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 invoice looks like in UBL XML — field by field, with a Slovak example.

The big idea

A Peppol invoice is an XML document that follows a strictly defined structure. It's not a PDF — it's machine-readable data that the receiving system processes automatically. The format is UBL 2.1 (Universal Business Language, ISO/IEC 19845), a standardised XML vocabulary for business documents.

Your Access Point will need to:

  • Generate valid UBL invoices from your customers' data (or accept them from their accounting software)
  • Validate them against the Peppol BIS rules before sending
  • Parse incoming UBL invoices and deliver them to your customers
💡 Two syntaxes are allowed: UBL 2.1 XML (most common) and CII (Cross-Industry Invoice). UBL is used in ~95% of Peppol traffic. We focus on UBL.

The Business Groups (BG) model

The Peppol BIS spec organises invoice data into Business Groups (BG) and Business Terms (BT). Each field has a unique BT number. This numbering is consistent across all EU e-invoicing:

  • 0192 — SWIFT/BIC

Validation: Two layers

Every Peppol invoice goes through two validation stages:

1. XSD Schema validation

Checks the XML structure — are all required elements present? Are the data types correct? Is the nesting right? This is mechanical and handled by your XML parser.

2. Schematron rules

This is where the business logic lives. Schematron is a rule-based validation language. It catches errors like:

  • "If VAT category is 'Standard', the rate must be the country's standard rate"
  • "PayableAmount must equal TaxInclusiveAmount − PrepaidAmount + RoundingAmount"
  • "If InvoiceTypeCode is 384 (corrected invoice), a BillingReference to the original invoice is required"
  • "At least one of BuyerReference or PurchaseOrderReference must be present"

OpenPeppol publishes two Schematron files you must run:

  • CEN-EN16931-UBL.sch — the European standard rules (EN 16931)
  • PEPPOL-EN16931-UBL.sch — Peppol-specific additional rules

The two envelopes

The UBL XML above is the payload. Before it travels over AS4, it gets wrapped in two more layers:

AS4 Message (SOAP + WS-Security) └── SBDH (Standard Business Document Header) ├── Sender ID: 0088:SK2023456789 ├── Receiver ID: 0088:SK4498765432 ├── Document Type: urn:oasis:names:specification:ubl:schema:xsd:Invoice-2::Invoice##... └── Document: <Invoice>...</Invoice> (the UBL XML)

SBDH tells the receiving AP: "Who sent this, who should get it, what type of document is inside." This is essential for routing. More on this in Lesson 3 (AS4).
