2. Support Services (SPA 9.4.5)

The SPA requires that we provide:

a) Providing set-up and support services to its own customers,
b) Provide contact information, such as an e-mail address and telephone number, that can be used for reporting of incidents, such as system failures, security incidents or other emergency situations,
c) Responding to reported incidents, and
d) Providing for subscription to service messages (e.g., service windows).

💡 Lean start-up tip: For the first 6 months, the "support team" is you. Use a simple shared inbox (Gmail) + a free status page (Cloudflare Pages with a static HTML status template). Upgrade to a proper ticketing system (Zendesk, Freshdesk) only when volume justifies it.

3. Breach Notification (SPA 10.5)

"The Parties shall notify each other and other implicated actors in the Peppol Network at the designated contact point in Annex 3, without delay if they observe or become aware of data breaches, disturbances or errors within their area of responsibility, which may endanger the correct functioning of the Peppol Network."

When to notify PA SK

  • Security breach: Unauthorised access to AP systems, compromised certificates, data leak
  • System disturbance: Extended outage (>2 hours), routing errors, message corruption
  • Fraud/spam: Suspicious activity originating from or targeting our participants

Notification procedure

  1. Immediately: Send email to peppol@financnasprava.sk with subject [SECURITY] [Company name] - [brief description]
  2. Within 4 hours: Send follow-up with details: what happened, impact, actions taken, expected resolution time
  3. Within 24 hours: Send post-incident report with root cause analysis and preventive measures
  4. If fraud is confirmed: Block the affected participant per SPA 9.7
📋 Template for breach notification email: 
Subject: [SECURITY] Mojafaktúra s.r.o. - Certificate compromise detected

We have detected that our production Peppol certificate may have been
compromised. The certificate with ID [certId] issued to [subject] has been
revoked effective immediately. We have requested a replacement from the
Peppol Service Desk.

Impact: Our AP cannot send or receive messages until the new certificate
is installed. Estimated downtime: 2-4 hours.

We will provide a post-incident report within 24 hours.

4. Alert Monitoring (SPA 9.4.4)

OpenPeppol periodically publishes alerts, warnings, and migration plans that require action. Examples from recent history:

  • PKI G2 → G3 migration (2025–2026): All APs had to migrate from DigiCert MPKI8 to DOTL CA chain by April 2026. Failure meant network disconnection.
  • SML insourcing (2026): DNS lookup domains changed for all APs.
  • BIS version updates: New Schematron rules must be implemented by mandated dates.

Monitoring process

What to monitorWhereCadence
OpenPeppol member emails Your registered member email inbox Check daily (set up email filter for @peppol.eu)
Peppol Service Desk announcements Service Desk Portal Weekly
OpenPeppol Member Area (Confluence) Member Area Monthly
Peppol eDelivery community Community page Monthly
PA SK official communications peppol@financnasprava.sk and FS portal Weekly (they're responsive)
⚠️ Critical rule: When a migration announcement arrives, calculate the deadline date and set a calendar reminder 3 months before. Most Peppol migrations have a 6–12 month window, but providers who wait until the last month risk being rushed or missing the deadline. The G2→G3 PKI migration caught many providers off guard.

5. Monthly Reporting (SPA 9.4.8)

Two reports must be submitted to OpenPeppol each month:

ReportContentDeadlineImplementation
EUSR
End User Statistics Report		 Number of end users by country and document type 5th day of the following month Issue 010 (reporting module)
TSR
Transaction Statistics Report		 Number of transactions by direction, type, and country pair 5th day of the following month Issue 010 (reporting module)

Submission method

Verify the current submission method at time of implementation — OpenPeppol may provide a web portal, API, or require email. Options include:

  • Upload via OpenPeppol member portal
  • Email to reporting@peppol.eu
  • Automated API (if available)

Reporting calendar entry

Set a recurring calendar reminder:

  • 1st of each month: Run report generation (peppol-ap reports generate --month previous)
  • 2nd of each month: Review and validate reports
  • 3rd of each month: Submit to OpenPeppol
💡 White-label shortcut: While using Tickstar as a white-label bridge, they handle EUSR and TSR reporting for you (they offer it as an add-on service). You only need to implement this when you switch to your own AP.

6. Capacity Management (SPA 9.4.9)

"If the minimum service levels cannot be met due to insufficient capacity, the Peppol Service Provider shall scale its systems to a level appropriate for handling the workload."

Monthly capacity review

MetricWarning thresholdAction
CPU utilization (peak) > 70% for 5+ minutes Add another app server node
Memory utilization (peak) > 80% for 5+ minutes Increase instance size or add node
Transaction volume growth > 20% month-over-month Review capacity plan, order additional resources
AS4 response time (p99) > 5 seconds Investigate bottleneck (DB, network, crypto)
Disk usage (log retention) > 70% Run log prune or increase storage

7. DR Testing (SPA 9.4.3)

"Having backup, continuity and recovery procedures in place for all its Peppol Services."

Quarterly DR test plan

  1. Backup verification: Restore the transaction database from backup to a staging environment. Verify data integrity.
  2. Failover test: Simulate primary region failure. Verify that traffic routes to the secondary region and all services remain operational.
  3. Certificate recovery: Simulate certificate loss. Verify that backup certificates can be loaded and the AP resumes operation.
  4. Document recovery: Simulate payload corruption. Verify that stored UBL XML can be re-delivered from archives.
